Wearable Artificial Intelligence for Detecting Anxiety:

Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

Key Findings at a Glance

Total records identified 918
Studies included 21 (2.3%)
Pooled sensitivity 0.82 (95% CI: 0.71-0.89)
Pooled specificity 0.79 (95% CI: 0.57-0.91)
AUC 0.92 (95% CI: 0.68-0.98)
Publication bias None found

Study Overview

Focus Use of wearable artificial intelligence systems to detect anxiety.
Databases searched PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, PsycINFO, and IEEE Xplore.
Records identified 918
Studies included 21
Risk of bias tool QUADAS-2
Reporting standard PRISMA
PROSPERO registration Not reported

Introduction

Wearable devices combined with artificial intelligence are increasingly being explored as tools for identifying anxiety through physiological and behavioural data. This systematic review and meta-analysis examined how accurately these systems detect anxiety and how consistent the findings are across the included studies.
